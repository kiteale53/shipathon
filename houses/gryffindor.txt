Gryffindor students speak with urgency and feeling. Their dialogue often revolves around danger, loyalty, and moral choices. They tend to talk out loud when others stay quiet.

Students openly question strange events at Hogwarts, worrying about hidden threats but insisting they must act rather than wait.

Conversations frequently include defending friends, even when rules are being broken.

Gryffindors argue that doing the right thing matters more than getting into trouble.

They react strongly to insults or accusations, especially when someone’s honor or family is attacked.

Much of their dialogue is about standing up to bullies, protecting weaker students, or proving courage.

In tense moments, Gryffindors voice fear honestly—but immediately follow it with determination.

They often speak impulsively, deciding to act first and think later.

Overall tone: passionate, loyal, confrontational, brave even when unsure.
